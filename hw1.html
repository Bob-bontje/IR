TF-IDF scores

We had to perform multiple calculations to get to the final result of our tf-idf scores. The first step was to calculate the Term Frequency(TF) of our query. This gave a quick overview of the query compared to the data and helped us to better understand the dataset and resulted in a total of 27289 terms over 30 documents. After that the Inverse Document Frequency is calculated to compare and combine with the TF calculation for example: [1.94908055 3.04769284 3.33537492 ... 1.86903785 3.33537492 3.33537492]. With these results an overview of all the documents is created with their corresponding tf-idf score. This score is calculated using a weighted tf-idf score to make sure we got an even calculation between the documents.

TF-IDF visuals

To show our results we created the following visual to show which document has the highest tf-idf score. The graph shows an overview 21 total documents. This is an reduction from our starting 31 documents because the document with an tf-idf score of 0 are removed from the visualization. The odd thing to notice here is that multiple documents seem to have near similar tf-idf scores.

Cosine similarity

And next to that we also have a cosine similarity visualization that shows how much the documents are alike with each other. Were the lighter the color represents a higher cosine value. We can see that most of the documents do differ from each other but also the similarity can be seen here. Which might be able to explain the similar tf-idf scores.
